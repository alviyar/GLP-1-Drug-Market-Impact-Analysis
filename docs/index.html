Analysis Output

Key Findings

From five years of CMS data, cleaned and modeled across 8 GLP-1 drugs.

FINDING_01

Medicare Part D spending rose from ~$1B in 2019 to over $20B in 2023 — a 20× increase in four years.

FINDING_02

Beneficiaries grew 471%, from 535.7K to 3.06M — confirming demand-driven growth, not just price inflation.

FINDING_03

Diabetes drugs (Ozempic, Trulicity, Rybelsus) drove $18.22B in 2023 spend — all fully covered under Part D.

FINDING_04

Obesity drugs remain excluded by statute. 2023 spend: $233.72K — near-zero despite equivalent clinical efficacy.

FINDING_05

Ozempic's avg cost per claim grew 25.8% from 2019–2023, revealing price inflation on top of patient volume growth.

FINDING_06

Only 3 CMS outlier flags across the window — both tied to low-volume obesity drugs, validating data integrity.

A full Python → Power BI pipeline, from raw CMS data to a decision-ready dashboard.

Data Cleaning

Dropped non-relevant columns, removed 2,201 duplicate rows while retaining legitimate multi-row brand/generic entries. Nulls resolved by cause — zero-activity years filled with 0; CMS privacy-suppressed counts left as NaN.
